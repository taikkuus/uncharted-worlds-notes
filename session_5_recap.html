[02] DEPARTURE PREP — OUEIKARU BOARDS
ACCOMMODATIONS
The medbay submersion pool is confirmed serviceable. Oueikaru — a Kaluarga, third gender (matrix), semi-aquatic, two prehensile tails, ceremonial robes — boards with fluid grace and inspects the modifications. Verdict: "That'll be satisfactory." They do not need full immersion for the whole journey — a high-humidity cabin adjacent to the medbay will suffice day-to-day. Elias' tech crew dials the environmental controls up. Condensation visible on cabin walls. Automatic misters active.
ROUTING
Elias advises Neyla to plot a less-traveled route to avoid drawing attention. The crew agrees the priority is avoiding Crethul-heavy transit nodes. Neyla plots accordingly — clear of the direct station and its associated nodes. The Lovelace enters substrate transit. Today is Tuesday.
RESEARCH GOALS
In-transit crew assignments: Neyla and Kepler — get to know Oueikaru. K'thlx-kek — learn Pelhari culture ahead of arrival. Mara — research Thessavar: the Pelhari Continuance, protocol expectations, neighboring systems and factions. Elias — dig deeper into the 25-year Node 7V anomaly using travel data for comparison.
[03] FIRST NIGHT — DINNER WITH OUEIKARU

First evening in transit. The crew eats in the conference room / officers' mess. Elias's executive assistant prepares a protein stir fry from the ship's stores. Oueikaru brings their own nutrient paste — a specially formulated enzyme compound required during their current meditative state.

"I am aware of the smell. If you prefer that I eat in my cabin from now on, that is acceptable."
  • The nutrient paste has an overpowering, distinctive smell — present between every bite of everyone else's meal. The crew adjusts. Mostly.
  • K'thlx-kek offers Oueikaru a taste of crew food in return. Oueikaru declines — dietary restrictions during meditative state are strict. However, they invite K'thlx-kek to try the paste. K'thlx-kek accepts (face adversity, physique). The flavor is unexpectedly nostalgic — not unlike food given to young on their homeworld. Oueikaru notices the recognition. Something shifts between them.
  • Kepler asks about Oueikaru's family. Answer: they belong to a small crèche, raised collectively — biological parentage matters less than functional role (caregiver, playmate, etc.). Not back to their homeworld in twenty years.
  • A follow-up question is asked about why they haven't returned. Oueikaru: "I don't mean to be rude, but I will decline to answer that question. No ill will." The topic is closed without tension.
  • Mara's research results: The Pelhari Continuance (Thessavar's global government) prizes ritual in almost every aspect of life. Protocol is deeply embedded culturally. Spacefaring Pelhari are more relaxed, but planet-bound ones are sticklers. The crew needs to be careful about procedural missteps on arrival.
[04] ELIAS — NODE 7V ANOMALY INVESTIGATION

Elias accesses the dark sector of the station network to dig further into the 25-year anomaly. Partial success on the access roll — the session is not stealthy. An undeletable browser history of this query now exists. Poking further carries risk of being noticed.

  • Ship registration chains are locked and maintained at transit nodes. Charting the Lovelace's full transit history shows: there is no record of this ship ever being at Node 7V 25 years ago. No log. No approach. Nothing.
  • The Lovelace itself is barely 30 years old. The math does not work if it was a prior voyage.
  • While deep in the dark sector, an encrypted message arrives addressed to Elias. No timestamp.
  • Elias moves it to an air-gapped personal system — the same secure partition holding the Rusk recording — and decrypts it there.
MESSAGE CONTENTS: A single sentence.

"Too much knowledge is dangerous."

Signed with Elias's own initials. The message carries no timestamp. It is not clear when it was sent, or from when.

No follow-up was taken in-session. The message is held on Elias's personal encrypted partition. No one else has been told.

[05] UNKNOWN BIOSIGNATURE — "BENEDICT"

Several hours into transit, a blinking indicator appears on the ship's hollow display: an unknown biological signature aboard. It is in the cargo hold and moving rapidly through the ship's walls.

TRACKING
Mara reports the signature and tracks it on her display. Neyla can see it through her technologic awareness. Elias moves to intercept in engineering. The creature travels through walls, moving upward level by level — from cargo, through crew, toward command. Clifton identifies it acoustically: it has a clear destination, moving with purpose.
DISCOVERY
Mara reaches Oueikaru's cabin first and knocks. The door opens. Something drops from a ceiling vent — Oueikaru catches it in their hands reflexively. They look at it. They look at Mara. "This isn't mine." The creature is roughly the size of a tennis ball: translucent, gelatinous, two mobile eye spots, six small pseudopod limbs, a glowing amber core. Its pseudopods grip Oueikaru's hands briefly before releasing.
IDENTIFICATION
Kepler's internal recognition roll: expertise. The creature's gelatinous translucent form and bioluminescent core match something from session zero — a dormant egg found in the cargo hold. Kepler's internal monologue: oh, fuck. The egg hatched. The crew has been carrying this thing since before the campaign started. It has apparently been living in the ship's infrastructure — specifically in a smuggling compartment beneath the medbay — this entire time.
CONTAINMENT
The creature seems drawn toward Oueikaru — possibly toward the high-humidity, high-temperature environment of their cabin. Mara telekinetically encourages it toward the medbay instead. It settles when placed in an environment-controlled cabinet with humidity and temperature dialed up. Two eye spots orient toward Neyla. Oueikaru is asked: do you know what this is? Answer: "I do not. And in my current biological state, I am susceptible to unknown organisms." Secure containment confirmed.
NAMING
Kepler has apparently been calling it Benedict. The name sticks.
[06] CYBER INCIDENT — SYSTEMS FAILURE IN SUBSTRATE
⚠ ACTIVE THREAT: While still in substrate transit, non-critical ship systems begin cycling offline and back on at increasing frequency — lights flickering, elevator inoperative, door seals intermittent. The pattern has all the hallmarks of a cyberattack. No external signal detected. Origin: internal.
ASSESSMENT
Neyla diagnoses the situation: something internal is attacking ship systems. Not Benedict — Elias traces his path through the walls and finds no corrosion or damage consistent with the creature causing this. The attack is spreading and accelerating. The only reliable fix is a full system restart — but that means dropping out of substrate and landing somewhere.
NEYLA'S CALL
Neyla is alone with the decision — everyone else is dealing with their section of the ship. She suspects it may be a trap to force them out of substrate at a specific location. She exits anyway: "Better judgment says exit at the nearest node." She plots for a planet two light-hours out and engages the Astrophage drives to cut transit to 30 minutes.
CRASH SEQUENCE
The sudden deceleration hits hard. K'thlx-kek is thrown against a wall — major injury, minor stun debility. Numbness, noticeable lilt, clumsy until rested. Kepler scrambles to protect Benedict's container but is flung across the medbay, slams into the smuggling tank hatch, which closes on him. He activates his sealed helmet before taking water. Clifton is pinned against the elevator door by g-forces — no permanent damage, wind knocked out, suit absorbs impact.
POWER FAILURE
The power drops mid-braking burn. Elias and his tech crew — in engineering — lose 15 seconds of the braking burn. Neyla compensates on partial success: brings it down to survivable. The Lovelace takes major damage on landing — what would have been critical is walked back by Neyla's skill. Everyone checks on everyone else. Alive.
WHERE THEY ARE
Unknown rocky moon or planet. Low gravity — slightly less than standard. On the horizon, perhaps a mile out: several brown buildings. Some kind of settlement.
