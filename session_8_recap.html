[01] OPENING STATE

The Lovelace is in dock at Thessavar. The Long Carry is done. Oueikaru has been delivered. The pregenesis ceremony occurred during transit and was witnessed by the crew — waist-up, nobody gyrating, more like standing in water. The ship is expected to be in dock for approximately ten in-game weeks. The crew is free agents for the duration.

  • Thessavar has a zoned layout: a Galactic Market district for off-world visitors, a Pelhari settlement ring beyond it, and Spiritual markets in the outer ring. Most crew business can be conducted in the Galactic zone.
  • Known cargo aboard: Thelac rosin (class-three) and cryopumps and thermal condensate (class-three, from Kepler's wager payout). Superconducting fiber has already been sold at a prior station.
  • Thelac rosin is not straightforwardly legal on Thessavar but is not outright prohibited. A licensed intermediary is required to find a buyer.
[02] MARA — CYBERNETIC REPLACEMENT RESOLVED
ACQUISITION
Mara attempts to source a replacement pinky through the Galactic Market. The roll goes poorly. Elias assists with his commercial background. The deal still needs something to close it — Mara calls in a Coldwall favor rather than risk losing cargo. The favor is cleared.
OUTCOME
A cybernetic replacement is installed. Polymer skin synthesized to match Mara's complexion. Up close: a faint seam, reads as a hand wrinkle at distance. Three days recovery. Mara has her pinky back — cybernetic, with occasional phantom sensation.
[03] NEW NPC — GILVARA, PELHARI ATTACHÉ

Rep K sends contacts to locate a commercial intermediary familiar with Formicides or prior off-world dealings. The result is Gilvara, a Pelhari attaché in off-world commerce. First contact via screen call.

Fine velvety golden fur, almost invisible at distance. Large warm eyes. No external ears. Spotted patterning on her head and down her back. Business attire with an ornate tessellated-triangle necklace — each triangle engraved with a Pelhari pictogram. Formal in bearing, practically useful.
  • Gilvara confirms thelac rosin can be sold on Thessavar to buyers with the appropriate license. She will find one for a percentage fee.
  • She provides a +1 on the barter roll as intermediary. Worst-case outcomes are reduced by her involvement.
  • Rep K makes the roll with her bonus plus career modifier. Result: full success. The thelac rosin is sold. Gilvara's fee is folded into the transaction. Cargo cleared.
  • Gilvara continues to be useful: she offers to help the crew find work. They are interested.
[04] LOVELACE UPGRADE — ENERGY CANNON

The crew decides to use the cryopumps-and-condensate cargo to acquire an offensive weapon for the Lovelace. Their existing point-defense turrets handle small craft. They want something for larger threats. The Crethul are the implicit reference point.

SELECTION
Crew deliberates between energy cannons and a launcher. Clifton advocates energy cannons — effective against Crethul-scale threats, less useful against small fast craft (which the turrets already cover). Motion carries.
PLACEMENT
Debate over mounting location. Clifton initially suggests rear-facing for a running-fight posture. Elias argues center-top for a forward arc with swivel range. The GM notes that in space, orientation is relative — the ship can spin. Final placement: center-top, forward-facing primary arc, with swivel. Approximately 60-degree effective coverage.
ACQUISITION
Class-two weapon, consistent with the Lovelace's class. The cryopumps-and-condensate cargo is consumed in the transaction — Gilvara's network handles procurement. Cargo hold updated. Ship journal updated by Rep K.
NICKNAME
GM solicits a name. After deliberation: Class-Three Heavy Discharge Cannon, nicknamed "the Loudmouth."

The rifle is marked nonfunctional on the sheet.

[06] CONTRACT BOARD — THREE POSTINGS

The crew tells Gilvara they want work — specifically artifact retrieval, off-world, with some surface engagement. She filters accordingly and returns three options.

JOB 01 — THE GRAPHICAL CODEX
MEDIUM RISK UNDREL PRESERVATION SOCIETY / CONTINUUM
Client: Keth Aulric (Undrel — known to the crew from Session 1). Posting organization: Undrel Preservation Society, filed through the Continuum.

Artifact: A pre-Continuum navigation archive called the Graphical Codex. Approximately toaster-sized.

Location: Ruum 4 — a tidally locked moon. Surface temperatures on the sun-facing side exceed safe thresholds. The outpost sits on the terminator line with minimal atmosphere. The corridor to this system is unstable — a wild jump may be required.

Rate: Negotiable. The crew has prior history with Keth Aulric and could likely negotiate better terms.
JOB 02 — CRETHUL SEXTANT
HIGH RISK LEGALLY QUESTIONABLE
Client: Unknown non-Crethul party. The agent requesting it is not Crethul — they argue the artifact predates the current Crethul empire and is therefore not covered by Crethul cultural property law. Academically defensible. Practically complicated.

Artifact: A pre-theocratic Crethul ceremonial navigation instrument — a space sextant. Star-alignment device.

Rate: High-tier flat fee on delivery.

Complication: Requires transponder signal change en route. Known to most of the crew to be seriously illegal for civilian vessels. Rep K explicitly notes the crew is already on the Crethul's bad side. Voted down.
JOB 03 — NOSI WAYSTONES
ANONYMOUS CLOSEST LOCATION
Client: Anonymous. No contact details. Payment held by neutral third party — verifiable and guaranteed before departure, but not releasable until job completion. Once accepted, there is no one to contact.

Artifact: NOSI waystone markers — small columns of undetermined material. Described as positional notation. Part of an ancient survey expedition, seven markers total, never recovered. Species of origin unknown. Low interest from other parties in the posting system.

Location: Outer edge of the Thessavar system — beyond the range of Pelhari surveys of their own system. Closest of the three jobs.

Note: The crew flags that "NOSI" and the requesting species are unidentified. Clifton finds the obscurity appealing. Posting has very low engagement — no other parties appear to be competing for it.
