[01] SITUATION — DAMAGE ASSESSMENT

Session opens immediately after the crash. K'thlx-kek is still stunned from the landing impact — Kepler administers first aid (full success) and gets them mobile. The crew takes stock.

  • The ship is listing significantly — one or more landing struts bent or broken on impact.
  • Significant breach in the aft cargo hold — the hull has been holed. Not space-worthy until patched. Requires materials the tech crew does not currently have.
  • Food stores are gone — the crash jettisoned most of them. Two days' worth remains in the galley. This includes all of Oueikaru's nutrient paste supply.
  • The tech crew can execute the full system restart (to clear the cyberattack) in a couple of hours — but that's all they can do unassisted. Repair materials must come from elsewhere.
  • Comms with the ship are intermittent — the thelac spores muck up radio antenna transmissions. The antenna requires regular manual cleaning.
  • Vasken's Crossing is not Crethul space. The routing away from Crethul nodes held. This is a transit moon — primarily frequented by merchants, traders, and thelac harvesters.
[02] LOCATION — VASKEN'S CROSSING

Mara makes a full-success navigation assessment. Data point earned. The location is identified.

  • Vasken's Crossing — a rocky moon in a transit system. Orbits a gas giant visible and bright in the sky. No permanent population; transient-majority. The only reason anyone comes here is thelac spores.
  • Thelac (Velx-4) — a plant-fungus hybrid. Low mossy beds with stalks that release spore bursts when stepped on, then slowly retract. The spores fill the thin (non-breathable) atmosphere in a pinkish-orange haze. Mild psychoactive properties. The only natural source in known space. Refined into a mildly valuable rosin. Continuum has not explicitly legislated it — jurisdiction varies by destination.
  • The spores clog suit filters — sealed gear is mandatory outdoors. Filters require manual clearing every ten minutes or so without the advanced filtration units. Sealed suits are confirmed for all crew.
  • An outpost of several buildings sits approximately one mile away across the badlands terrain. Two landing pads, each with fuel tanks, crane, and cargo containers. The buildings include a cantina, a freight brokerage, a medical clinic, and a docking authority office.
[03] THE TRAVERSE — SPORE FIELDS & INFECTED
PREP
Clifton scouts the terrain and identifies unknown life form signals trailing the group — not crew, not ship. Something biological is following them out in the spore fields. Elias uses his expedition kit in advance and locates a cache of six advanced filter units — gear from a prior survey mission into the thelac fields. Seven crew members, six filters. One person goes without. Clifton draws the short straw, facing worsening conditions.
FIRST BODY
Elias trips setting up a motion sensor tripod and faceplants into a thelac patch. Wiping spores off his faceplate, he finds himself looking at another faceplate — cracked down the middle, pressed into the ground. A desiccated body in a torn suit. Filaments from the thelac are moving into or out of it. One of the eyes in the husk tracks toward him. The crew is informed. Nobody touches it.
CONTACT
Kepler reappears from wherever he'd wandered off to and spots it first: another suited figure in the mid-distance, moving erratically. Face shield completely destroyed — thelac filaments visible inside the suit. It registers the crew and immediately charges. Kepler runs. Clifton fires — takes the left leg off below the knee with a penetrating round. The husk drops but continues dragging itself forward on its hands. Still coming.
SECOND INFECTED
While Clifton is watching the crawler, the desiccated body next to Elias begins to move — filaments snapping taut as it pushes itself upright from the ground. Elias draws his sniper rifle, flips backward to create distance, and fires point-blank into the head. Full detonation: thick blood and spores spray through the cracked helmet in both directions. The rifle jams immediately — the spore discharge has fouled the internal mechanisms. Elias is splattered but uninjured.
RESOLUTION
Clifton finishes the crawler with follow-up shots. It drops face-first. The badges on the suits match the emblem on the advanced filter units — these were part of the same survey crew that left the cache. Six filters found; six crew unaccounted for. Two encountered, both now down. Four more potentially out in the spore fields. The crew picks up the pace. All make it to the outpost without further incident.
[04] VASKEN'S CROSSING OUTPOST — ARRIVAL

The crew enters through a decontamination airlock — pressurized air jets blast thelac dust off suits from multiple angles over about a minute. Inside: a small cantina with a few traders drinking and smoking. A human woman behind the bar — looks like she runs the place.

"Alright. Go ahead and have a seat so I can — grab yourself a drink if you like."
  • Bar menu: mushroom farm output — lasagna, chili, protein stir fry. Simulated meats. Alcohol and water. A round of shots is poured for the crew. Mara gains +1 ongoing to influence rolls for the next hour (inhibitions loosened). No noted harm otherwise.
  • Brodha — the human woman running the cantina. De facto authority figure at the outpost. Directs the crew to relevant specialists: Prevek (docking authority / impound) for ship repair materials; Holst (freight broker) for acquisition and trade; Yuleph (medic) for Oueikaru's dietary needs.
  • Brodha mentions a crew that went out a few days ago and hasn't checked in. Doesn't work for the outpost directly — usually they do check in. The suit emblems from the infected match what the crew describes.
  • A Pelhari courier ship has just docked on the west pad. Currently working through Vasken's Crossing docking protocols — Brodha: "sticklers for protocol and ritual." Crew can approach them once the formalities clear, in a couple of hours.

[06] CRITICAL — OUEIKARU NUTRIENT SUPPLY
⚠ MISSION RISK: Oueikaru is in a pre-genesis biological state requiring a strict, rare-ingredient nutrient regimen maintained for months. This diet is not optional — breaking it does not kill them immediately, but it terminates the pregenesis state, failing the primary mission objective. Stasis is not a viable workaround for the same reason.
MEDIC (YULEPH)
Yuleph confirms she can synthesize some nutrient paste but her precursor stock is extremely limited. Estimate: one meal available within an hour or two, two more overnight, then depleted. She checks the supply chain — the ingredient list Oueikaru provides is long and the items are rare. She will flag any incoming traders who might carry them.
PELHARI COURIER
K'thlx-kek approaches the Pelhari crew after they complete their arrival ritual — correctly waiting for them to be seated and fully settled before approaching, mirroring what elements of their threshold protocol they observed. The captain assesses K'thlx-kek at length — possibly never having seen a Formicide, possibly a prior negative impression of the species. Suspicious but not hostile.
THE CAPTAIN'S RESPONSE
K'thlx-kek explains the situation: transporting Oueikaru to Thessavar, crashed, nutrient supply lost. The captain responds: they have the supplies Oueikaru needs — but they are not for the crew. Clarification: the supplies may already be earmarked, or the captain objects to the nature of the transit, or both. The Pelhari captain is not saying no outright — but he is not offering a path forward either.
NEGOTIATION
Elias considers a bribe (acquisition roll). K'thlx-kek tries a partial influence approach — business framing: can't complete the job, can't get paid, the procedure is legitimate if uncommon. Partial success. The Pelhari captain says they will provide the supplies — at a cost. He sends a file to the crew's handhelds and says to contact his ship tomorrow once they've reviewed it. The contents of the file are not revealed in-session. The GM defers to next session: "the Pelhari blood sports."
