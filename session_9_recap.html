[01] CONTRACT SELECTION — NOSI WAYSTONES

The absent player's vote resolved the session 8 tie: Job 3 — the anonymous NOSI waystone contract. Job 2 (Crethul sextant, transponder change required) was eliminated early. Job 1 (Keth Aulric, unstable corridor, extreme environment) was the main alternative. The NOSI job won on proximity and relative simplicity of the brief.

  • Client: Anonymous. No contact. Payment held by neutral third party — verifiable, released on delivery.
  • Target: NOSI waystone markers. Seven pillars, pentagonal cross-section, carved stone, placed by an ancient survey expedition. Never recovered. Species of origin unknown at time of contract acceptance.
  • Location: Tazek Null — a tidally locked moon orbiting the gas giant Tazek. Approximately the same distance from its star as Venus is from ours. The planet itself is completely inhospitable. The moon: half bad.
  • Condition discovered on arrival: The ruins are on the sun-facing side — survivable for a few days but irradiated. The crew opts for a dark-side approach: tighter landing zone, harder entry, but less radiation exposure.
[02] TRANSIT — BENEDICT UPDATE / CRAMPED QUARTERS
NAVIGATION
Neyla uses her navigation skill to chart a route avoiding the Crethul (referred to in-session as the Prefel — confirm canonical spelling). The route is clean. No entanglements.
CRAMPED QUARTERS
GM calls a Cramped Quarters roll — Mara and Neyla, whose workstations share the same floor. Mara asks Neyla what she's afraid of. Neyla reveals a concern that long-term Substrate exposure may interfere with her integrated nanosystems. Her species may have a specific vulnerability. This has been a quiet fear she carries into every transit.
BENEDICT — STATUS
Kepler checks in. Benedict has been out of the cage intermittently during dock time — Kepler admits he let it roam a few times. Benedict has visibly grown: from palm-sized at last check to roughly two-fists / breadbox size. Development cycle appears rapid. New feature: cilia on the underside, allowing it to crawl along surfaces quickly. Behaviorally: moody, prone to short meltdowns — the GM describes this as a terrible-twos phase rather than true aggression. Benedict has learned which things on the ship are off-limits and which aren't. Daily trips to the recycler to fish out polymer waste are a highlight of its routine. The cage is no longer adequate containment but the crew hasn't formally released it.
[03] LANDING — TAZEK NULL (DARK SIDE)
APPROACH
Neyla and Mara attempt to find a tight dark-side landing spot rather than using the larger but irradiated sun-side plain. Mara assists via interface/metal. Failed roll. Elias rolls Shields Up to absorb the damage. Partial success. The Lovelace comes down hard: minor damage to the cargo bay — landing struts buckle on impact. Cargo bay doors still functional. The ship can be repaired without a full spaceport visit — crew or tech crew can handle it with downtime.
EVA BRIEFING
The moon is irradiated to its core — both sides. EVA suits protect against radiation but power cells burn out faster than normal. Each crew member has four power cells. Each cell lasts approximately 45 minutes under normal conditions — but conditions are not normal. The real-life timer starts the moment they set foot outside. Losing cells to anomalies or failures shortens the window. The crew deploys in EVA suits. Rep K's vehicle — the Quad Walker — is too large for the lava tube entrance.
[04] THE RUINS — TAZEK NULL LAVA TUBES

The lava tube network is a naturally formed system, widening and narrowing at intervals. Fifteen minutes from the landing site, the crew reaches the entrance.

ENTRANCE ANOMALY
Technology fused into the cave wall near the entrance — not matching any known design. Elias and Kepler attempt to assess it. Both fail. Time is lost from the timer. The device drains power cells when approached — this is understood after the fact, not before. The crew pushes past it, losing cell charge in the process.
THE GRAY MASS
In a side crack off the main passage, Elias finds a gray organic mass, approximately 5.5 meters. Initially mistaken for rock. Not rock. He photographs it and transmits metadata to the group. Kepler reads the metadata with expertise — full success.
ASSESSMENT RESULT — THE GRAY MASS:

Outer layer: polyphenylene ether analog providing photosensitivity. Skeletal deposits: barium-strontium mix. Soft tissue outer skin that temporarily absorbed Elias's flashlight beam — the glow faded quickly.

Classification: photophoretic species — eats light. Biological.

Identity: One of the Nosi. The species that posted the job. Apparently nobody knows what they look like. Now the crew does — or did. This one is not confirmed dead. There is something underneath the outer mass that is not stone and is not the creature's normal biology.
THE OBJECT BELOW
Teamsters sent in to retrieve whatever is under the mass. They surface with a large tablet, roughly iPad-sized, spongy and leathery on the outside. No visible electronics. Clifton has the teamsters run it through multiple light spectra. Under ultraviolet, strange text becomes visible — the same symbols found on the waystone pillars. Mathematical cipher. It contains the key to the waystones.
THE WARDEN
The passage collapse is cleared by Mara (telekinesis — failed), Clifton commanding the teamsters (influence — partial success; one teamster dies in the process), and Elias working from the far side using his Faster and Harder skills. The passage opens.
NOTE: Rep K probes one of the destroyed warden orbs to assess it. This triggers a sustained 75-second psychic feedback loop — a piercing noise directly in everyone's heads. Power cells drain. Everyone drops to their final cell. 45 minutes maximum remaining.
[05] THE WARDEN — COMBAT
// REP K'THLX-KEK DEFEATS THE WARDEN

The main chamber contains five large boulders in its center. Clifton shoots one. It activates — the boulders are hollow, each containing a red energy core. Five orbs plus a central node. A defense system comes online, orbiting the chamber.

CLIFTON
Shoots the central red core. Partial success via Tactics — chooses to take the consequence himself. A boulder swings through and hits him. Minor injury — Intense Pain debility (clumsy, face adversity on actions). One orb destroyed.
MARA
Uses Launch to hurl a rock shard through two orbs simultaneously — the fragment punches through the first and carries its red core into the second. Full success. Two orbs down. Three remain. The warden becomes more agile. It opens fire on the group. Mara takes minor damage (burns).
NEYLA
Caught in the energy backlash from Mara's attack. Major damage — burns.
REP K
Flies up and smashes the final orb down with a staff. Partial success. Warden destroyed. Collateral damage: the strike loosens enough boulders to partially collapse the exit passage behind the group, separating Elias and some teamsters from the main party.
[06] THE CHAMBER — SOLVING THE WAYSTONES
// KEPLER FACES THE WAYSTONES
THE ROOM
Beyond the warden's chamber: a carved stone floor — not natural. Seven pentagonal pillars, approximately 1.5 meters tall, arranged in an arc against the back wall. Each pillar has a different engraved symbol on its top face. The symbols match those on the Nosi tablet recovered from beneath the gray mass.
THE CIPHER
Clifton identifies the symbol system first: a base-five radially symmetrical counting system. Seven pillars numbered one through seven. The tablet contains a mathematical cipher — a combination expressed as pairs. The pillars must be rotated simultaneously in matched pairs. The Nosi, being physically pliable, could do this alone. The crew has to double-team each pair.
EXECUTION
Kepler takes the primary role — Face Adversity with Metal. First pair: success. Second pair: Kepler takes severe stun damage — partial paralysis of the right side of his body. Clifton takes over with Metal. Remaining pairs completed. Five waystones snap free. Two are passed through the rubble to Elias. The waystones are loose — the job is done.
RETRIEVED: 7 Nosi waystone markers (pentagonal stone pillars, ~1.5m, ancient survey markers, unknown material). Also retrieved: 1 Nosi tablet (leathery, UV-reactive, contains base-five cipher — not part of the job brief, taken by the crew).
[07] EXTRACTION
TELEPORT
Clifton teleports most of the group back through the collapsed section. Partial success — can get everyone but one person. Rep K left behind. Neyla, carrying burns and wearing lighter EVA armor, squeezes through the rubble. Mara uses telekinesis to assist (physique-based). Full success. Neyla clears the passage. Rep K faces adversity with physique to follow — rolls well. The group is through.
EXIT WINDOW
The crew reaches the surface with 15 minutes remaining on the last power cell. The entrance anomaly (power-drain device) presumably costs them some on the way out — the exact hit is not quantified but they make it back to the Quad Walker and return to the Lovelace. Job done. Everyone alive.
