Session 1 — Node 7V, Lane 7-Veth

Mission Brief

The Lovelace was tasked under TAW Infrastructure Coordination Division auxiliary authorization (ICD-VER-2251-0394) to conduct a physical inspection of transit Node 7V on Lane 7-Veth. The node had returned an anomalous internal signal, classified by Continuum Infrastructure Management as unresolved, non-critical, flagged for inspection. No further detail was provided.

Approach

Node 7V presented as a spherical structure roughly the size of an office building — unmanned, unremarkable. At 50 kilometers, the transponder acknowledged approach as normal. Maintenance manifest loaded automatically: last physical inspection fourteen months prior, all tolerances within normal range, no anomalies on record. The node was not flagging itself. Whatever made this assignment necessary had been noticed elsewhere.

A Crethul warship was already present, holding position at distance with the node between itself and the Lovelace. It did not respond to hails on general frequencies.

Lockdown

Attempts to remotely query the node's diagnostic logs triggered an unexpected response. The node's security system interpreted the probe as hostile and entered full lockdown, broadcasting a general distress signal. The Crethul warship maintained position. Remote access was now closed.

The crew assessed the situation and decided to board.

Boarding Action

The Lovelace docked at a node maintenance hardpoint and the crew entered via docking tube in EVA suits. Life support aboard was minimal. A laser grid had been activated inside. They were met by a Crethul boarding party: armored religious warriors carrying energy weapons and heavy ceremonial maces, the mace heads designed to open and expose internal energy cores.

A firefight followed in close quarters, low gravity, across the node's access corridors. The engagement was costly on both sides.

  • Elias sustained a severe injury — fractured arm from a deflected pulse shot.

  • Mara sustained a major injury — dislocated shoulder from an energy lash.

  • Clifton sustained a major injury — broken rib from a dying warrior's final charge.

The Crethul boarding party was eliminated. Their leader — carrying a consecrated staff — was killed in the engagement. Post-combat assessment of his insignia indicated he held significantly higher rank than a standard temple guardian. He was an officer of the Righteous Fury, the Crethul warship on station.

Shortly after the fighting ended, the Lovelace relayed a message: the Righteous Fury had departed the sector without attempting to recover its people.

Log Retrieval

Neyla accessed the node's physical terminal, cleared the distress signal, and downloaded the full log archive. The critical finding was as follows.

Twenty-five years ago, the node recorded a handshake return from the Lovelace's own transponder ID — before the Lovelace was present. When the crew approached within 30 kilometers during this mission, the standard close-approach protocol executed normally: transponder out, node returns it. Then the node returned it a second time. Same vessel. Same registration. Timestamp: twenty-five years in the past.

The node's internal classification of this event: UNRESOLVED. Flagged yellow. Inspected. No escalation recommended. No corresponding log entry exists for twenty-five years ago.

Either it was scrubbed. Or it hasn't happened yet.

The full log archive was uploaded to the Lovelace. Neyla also confirmed no security logs from the Crethul approach were recoverable — the only data pulled was their vessel registration. The warship: the Righteous Fury.

Withdrawal

The crew recovered a Crethul pulse rifle, the boarding leader's ceremonial staff, and a damaged mechanical unit of unknown origin found aboard the node. Crethul bodies were disposed of using the ship's recycler. The crew departed back through the anchor node.

The return transit took approximately four hours. Minor injuries cleared in transit. Severe injuries did not.

Aftermath

Upon arrival at Verath Station, Elias was transferred to a medical facility in Sector 379 for surgery. He woke up to find Drindl — a former business partner — waiting at his bedside. Drindl believes Elias owes him considerably more than the courts determined. The nature of his visit was not resolved before the session closed.

Outstanding Threads

  • One debt owed to the Crethul Empire. The boarding leader's rank makes this non-trivial.

  • The 25-year transponder anomaly — logged, unresolved, and in the crew's possession.

  • The mechanical unit recovered from the node. Origin and function unknown.

  • Drindl. His claim. His timing.
