[01] CONTEXT — THE JOB

The crew of the TAW-CVS Lovelace were contracted — through an anonymous TAW intermediary — to investigate an anomalous signal returned by Node 7V, a substrate transit node in the Verath corridor system. The node is a sphere roughly the size of an office building, unmanned, self-maintaining. A standard diagnostic cycle three days prior had flagged something unusual. No escalation had been filed.

  • Node 7V's last physical inspection was fourteen months ago. All tolerances were within normal range. No anomalies on record — the system did not flag itself.
  • The anomaly was identified as unusual by outside observers, not by the node's own systems.
  • Payment: a class-two cargo asset (superconducting fiber) and a favor with the Continuum intermediary. No follow-up contact information provided.
[02] APPROACH — FIRST CONTACT
50 KM OUT
Standard transponder handshake at 50 km. Node acknowledges approach, returns maintenance manifest. Last inspection fourteen months ago. Everything nominal. No logged anomaly — the strange signal is not visible from outside. A Crethul warship is already in the area, matching the Lovelace's distance from the opposite side of the node.
30 KM OUT
As the Lovelace closes to 30 km, the standard close-approach protocol fires: transponder ID goes out, node receives it, returns it. Normal. Then it returns it again — same ID, same ship, same registration — timestamped twenty-five years ago. Assessment rolls go poorly. Node security goes into full lockdown. Physical boarding is now the only option.
CRETHUL CONTACT
Attempts to hail the Crethul warship on general frequencies go unanswered. The representative broadcasts an open message explaining the anomaly and the intent to physically inspect. No response. Mara sets up sensor countermeasures to obscure the Lovelace's activities from the warship — partial success. One crew member must stay aboard to monitor.
[03] BOARDING — NODE 7V INTERIOR
DOCKING
The Lovelace's shuttle extends a docking tube to the node's external port. No life support inside — EVA suits required. The node is spinning; gravity is present but minimal. Cold, dark, and pressureless beyond their suits. Elias manages the door with infiltration tools — nearly electrocutes himself; Neyla catches it and turns the failure into a partial success. Door opens.
FIRST CONTACT
Inside: a broken window visible through a corridor. And sounds from another direction — a door opening on the opposite end of the node. The crew is not alone. Crethul wardens appear — a group of them, armed, moving toward the party with weapons raised. They fire a warning plasma blast.
"We do not recognize TAW authority here. This is a sacred space and we are protecting it. We are charged with defending the holy substrate. You are interlopers."

The representative attempts to reason with them, citing the Continuum concentrator who authorized the investigation. The Crethul wardens are unmoved. Combat begins.

[04] COMBAT — NODE INTERIOR
OPENING
Elias opens his faceplate to fire his laser eye across a group of wardens — cuts them down but takes a plasma hit in the process. Major injury. Clifton uses his penetrating rifle and rapid fire to engage two wardens in the corridor, bracing against the wall for positioning; clips one, sends another venting oxygen and stumbling away. The suit-venting warden makes a final fanatical charge — and dies of exertion. Clifton takes minor damage.
MID-COMBAT
Mara uses telekinesis to launch a fallen warden's energy mace into a second warden's helmet, cracking the faceplate and burning his face. She takes an energy lash to her arm — shoulder dislocated. Major injury. K'thlx-kek attempts to command the remaining wardens to subdue their leader. They fire on Elias instead. Elias braces; takes the major damage — rolls up to severe injury on his arm.
LEADER
Clifton teleports to flanking position and opens fire on the Crethul concentrator (the leader). Partial success — the concentrator deflects most bolts with his staff but one lands. Elias grabs a fallen warden's pulse rifle and fires — the concentrator deflects a bolt back at Elias, who braces. Elias takes severe damage. Neyla accesses the terminal at the back of the node through her technologic ability — takes mental backlash, major injury — but gains control of the system.
RESOLUTION
Neyla shuts off the distress signal and unlocks the system. Wardens are defeated. The concentrator is down. The Crethul warship, having apparently detected the leader's status, leaves the sector without attempting retrieval. The crew secures the node.
[05] NODE DATA — WHAT THE LOGS SHOW

With the system unlocked, Neyla accesses the full node log. The diagnostic record is clear until a single flagged entry:

Standard diagnostic cycle — all metrics nominal. One yellow flag: HANDSHAKE SEQUENCE — RETURN SIGNAL ORIGIN: INTERNAL. Duration: 28 seconds. Classification: UNRESOLVED. Disposition: no escalation — flagged for inspection.
  • When the Lovelace approached to 30 km, its transponder ID was sent to the node, returned — and then returned again, timestamped twenty-five years ago. Same vessel. Same registration. Current owners.
  • The crew searches the ship's own logs for a corresponding entry from 25 years ago. There is none. The log from that period is clean — no record that this signal was ever sent.
  • The previous ship owners were independent haulers. Chain of custody is traceable but the logs from their tenure were cleared — a fresh slate was given at transfer.
  • Elias: "Either it was scrubbed. Or it hasn't happened yet."
  • Full node logs downloaded to the Lovelace. A data point is recorded: NODE 7V ANOMALY.
[06] INCIDENT — CRETHUL CONCENTRATOR

Among the fallen Crethul, the concentrator (their leader) is identified by a marking on his person as someone of greater importance than a standard warden. The warship was identified via automatic approach protocol exchange as the Righteous Fury. The Crethul Empire's leadership likely knows their man is dead.

  • The Crethul have no Continuum council seat and are a known nuisance — 17 going on 18 formal grievances filed regarding substrate travel.
  • The Crethul treat substrate nodes as sacred ground. The crew was warned away; they boarded anyway. A Crethul pulse rifle was acquired.
  • Corpses were disposed of via the Lovelace's recycler. Ship logs were cleared to remove the boarding record.
  • The Crethul warship left without attempting recovery. The crew's working theory: the concentrator was high-ranking enough that his death was detected remotely.

⚠ CONSEQUENCE: The crew owes 1 DEBT to the Crethul Empire. When debt accumulates sufficiently, the assassins start coming.
